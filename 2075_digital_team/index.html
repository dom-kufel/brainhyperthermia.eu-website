Overview

Monte Carlo methods

Stochastic models of synaptic transmission are much simpler than detailed analytical 3D diffusion methods, but they may be as accurate.

Medical Applications

In simulations there is a possiblity to include different drugs or treatment methods to check whether hyperthermia may be prevented.

Markov Model kinetics methods

Markov models are useful and well experimentally tested methods for detailed analysis of temperature effects on synapse receptors.

Phase Plane Analysis

Phase portraits of differential equations are plotted to show how various set of parameters for differential equations can affect there solution.

Episodic Memory modelling

First model of temperature effects on synapses allows to research hyperthermia effects on neural dynamics, like encoding and recalling of episodic memory.

Local Field Potentials

To validate created model with experimental data, process of reproducing of emipirically measured field potentials was run and showed clear qualitatevly consistency with experimental data.

Why this is important?

Brain Hyperthermia

Hot topic in today's medicine

  • NEUROINFORMATICS
  • MEDICINE
  • NEUROBIOLOGY

Creating the model in computational neuroscience, we would like it to be easily scallable for different temperatures.

My model provides highly-optimized model for neural networks simulation including different temperatures.

Many medical studies suggest that hyperthermia can be an important source of brain damage in various neurological disorders like: traumatic brain injury, hot-water epilepsy, febrile seizures etc.

My model for the first time allows simplified computational analysis of brain hyperthermia. In the future it might allow to reduce neuronal damage in anti-cancer hyperthermia strategies and improve treatment methods of certain hyperthermia-related neurological disorders.

Inconsistent and elusive experimental results on topic of hyperthermia in brain suggest that mathematical model of temperature effects on neurons may be a strong argument supporting one of the theses about real neurobiological mechanism of hyperthermia.
